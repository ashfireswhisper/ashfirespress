What Just Sit With Me Is About

Just Sit With Me is a short, personal book about failed understanding-how it can leave people lonely, destabilized, and unsure of their own reality.

Drawing from lived experience, neurodivergent insight, and original research, M. Ian Niad explores the difference between hearing someone's words and actually receiving what those words are trying to carry.

This book is for anyone who has ever needed someone to stop fixing, stop explaining, and simply sit with them.

Workbook

Just Sit With Me: Reflection Workbook

A guided companion for people who have felt misunderstood before they felt heard.

Through 31 thoughtful exercises, the workbook explores what understanding means, why conversations break down, what helps people feel safe enough to open up, and what meaningful support can actually feel like.

Each section connects to the themes of Just Sit With Me: When Understanding Is Misunderstood, but it can also be used on its own. Write a little or a lot. Skip what does not fit. Return when you are ready.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Just Sit With Me about?

It is about what happens when someone’s lived experience is heard, corrected, interpreted, or explained away before it has been fully received.

Is the workbook separate from the book, and do I need to read the book first?

The workbook is a separate companion, but you do not need to read the book first. It can be used on its own or alongside the book for deeper reflection.

Who is this for?

It is for anyone who has felt misunderstood, over-explained themselves, struggled to feel heard, or wanted more thoughtful ways to understand themselves and others.

How should I use the workbook?

Move at your own pace. Write a little or a lot, skip anything that does not fit, and return when you are ready.
